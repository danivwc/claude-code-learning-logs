# 2026-03-24 — Day 2: The Quiet Days Before the Noise

> Danielle Vantini | Non-Technical AI Founder
> DanielleVantini.com | @danivwc

## What I Was Trying to Do

Keep absorbing the foundation before opening any terminal. Day 2 was more coaching sessions, more concepts, more silence in the system.

## What Actually Happened

Nothing shipped. No files. No agents. No commits. The system did not exist yet. What existed was a mental model being built one concept at a time — CLAUDE.md as a rulebook, agents as specialized terminals, MCPs as external connections, the Lethal Trifecta as the thing that quietly breaks agent behavior.

I kept asking myself: should I just open the terminal already? And I kept answering: not yet.

## What I Figured Out

**Invisible work is not the same as unimportant work.** You can spend a whole day on a non-technical AI project without producing a single file and still have the most productive day of the week. The concepts you absorb before the first build become the decisions you make without being told later.

The rule I kept hearing — "give Claude the whole problem, not step-by-step instructions" — made no sense to me on Day 1. On Day 2 it started to click. By Day 3 it was how I worked.

## What This Means for Non-Technical Founders

If you are doing it right, your early days look empty in your file system and full in your head. That is the correct ratio. The tool will wait. Your comprehension window will not. Use it.

## Next Step

Write down the one question per coaching session that I most need answered before I can build. Keep that list short and honest.
