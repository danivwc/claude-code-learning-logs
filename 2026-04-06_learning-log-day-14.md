## WHAT I UNDERSTOOD TODAY

- **Clearing context makes agents forget everything.** When you clear the conversation window, the agent starts fresh. It does not remember what you built, decided, or explained. This is not a bug — it is how the system works. Understanding it changes how you end every session.

- **Long conversations make agents smarter as they go.** The longer you work with an agent in a single session, the more context it holds, the better its answers become. The conversation itself is the intelligence. When you clear it, you lose that.

- **The solution is the end-of-session summary.** Before closing any long session: ask the agent to "summarize everything in a structured way." Save that summary to the agent's memory folder. The next session opens with full context, not a blank slate. This is the right protocol.

- **You can instruct agents to save the full conversation to memory.** When you build this into the agent's CLAUDE.md, it becomes automatic — the agent captures its own context at the end of every session without being asked. Linear understanding across sessions.

- **Competitive research confirmed the Blue Ocean.** The gap is real. Non-technical founders who want to build AI operations infrastructure have nowhere to go right now. The market exists. The product fits. The only question is execution speed.

- **Website pre-launch scan is a checkpoint, not a celebration.** All sections have copy and photos — that is good. But "best mentorship" section needs content, and more photos of Danielle are needed before full launch. Knowing this now costs nothing. Knowing it after launch costs audience.
